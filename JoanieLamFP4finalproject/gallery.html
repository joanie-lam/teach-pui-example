Wide-Legged Forward Bend Pose

How To:
Deep breath in and stand up tall while walk your feet out wide. Point your toes out slightly. Hinge at your hips and fold slightly forward gradually. Gently bring your hands to the mat directly under your shoulders. Bend and soften your knees slowly in order to release your head and neck. Roll up to a standing position.
Purpose of Pose:
Stretches the muscles in the back, hamstrings and calves.

Forward Fold Pose

How To:
Stand up tall and adjust with your feet hips-width distance apart close to the distance as your shoulder width. Relax your knees and gradually fold your body forward. Allow your arms, head and neck to drop heavily slowly. Place one hand to your heart and one hand to your belly. Close your eyes and take a deep breath.
Purpose of Pose:
Stretches the calves and hamstrings. Release tension in the head, neck and shoulders, and soothe the entire body.

Legs Up the Wall Pose

How To:
Place a block horizontally under your tailbone. Allow your entire body weight to rest on the block and extend your legs to the sky. Allow your feet to gently open. Flip your palms face down. Roll your shoulders down your back. Rest your entire body and close your eyes for a deep breath.
Optional:
Try this pose against on a wall. Bring your tailbone flush with the baseboard. Extend your legs up the wall and allow your arms to rest by your side, palms keep facing up.
Purpose of Pose:
Stretches the legs and back. Calm your body and mind.

Lizard Pose

How To:
Begin from downward-facing dog post. Step your left foot forward to a deep lunge and drop your right knee to the floor. Your feet should be far enough apart that your left foot is flat. Find a comfortable arm position and place long arms or forearms on a block. Hold for 10 deep breaths. Step back slowly to downward-facing dog pose and then switch legs.
Purpose of Pose:
Opens the hips and stretches the hamstrings. Opens the shoulder and chest muscles.

Supported Bridge Pose

How To:
Lie flat on your entire back. Place your feet flat and hips-width apart. Press down into your feet to lift your hips. Turn a yoga block to the tallest point and place it under your hips. Your body should feel supported and comfortable. Relax your arms on the ground along your sides with your palms facing up.
Optional:
Lift your arms overhead and allow them to rest next to your ears.
Purpose of Pose:
Stretches the entire front-side of your body and strengthens entire back.

Sphinx Pose

How To:
Lie flat on your stomach. Place your elbows under your shoulders and then begin to lift up your chest. Draw your shoulders down your back and press your forearms down. Look forward and allow your chin to drop slightly.
Purpose of Pose:
Stretches the abdominal muscles, opens the chest and shoulders and strengthens the spine.
